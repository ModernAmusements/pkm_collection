In the Pokémon TCG Pocket, Trainer cards are split into three main categories:

⸻

1) Item

Fast, one-time effects.
	•	Can be played freely during your turn (unless restricted by card text).
	•	Go to the discard pile after use.
	•	Examples: Potion (heal), Switch (swap Active Pokémon), Rare Candy (skip evolution stage).

Reasoning: Items create tempo and tactical flexibility without consuming your Supporter for the turn.

⸻

2) Supporter

Powerful one-per-turn Trainers.
	•	You may play only 1 Supporter per turn.
	•	Usually strong draw, disruption, or gust effects.
	•	Examples: Professor’s Research, Boss’s Orders, Marnie.

Reasoning: The once-per-turn limit balances their high impact.

⸻

3) Stadium

Global field effects.
	•	Placed next to the play area.
	•	Only one Stadium can be in play at a time.
	•	Replaced (discarded) when another Stadium is played.

Reasoning: Stadiums shape the overall game state and affect both players.

⸻

Older / Special Subtypes (format-dependent)
	•	Tool cards (now typically Pokémon Tools, attached to Pokémon; previously printed under Trainer).
	•	ACE SPEC (powerful Items; only 1 per deck, in specific formats).
